What is K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN)?

K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) is a simple, supervised machine learning algorithm used for classification and regression tasks. It works by comparing a new data point to its closest “neighbors” in the training dataset and classifies it based on the majority class among those neighbors.

In this project, KNN was applied to identify whether peer influence among students is Positive, Neutral, or Negative based on real academic and behavioral attributes.

What I Did & How I Did It

Using the KNN_PeerInfluence_RealFeatures dataset, the model was trained to analyze the relationship between student characteristics and their social influence outcomes.

FEATURE_COLS = ['ParentalInvolvement', 'SchoolType', 'MotivationLevel']

The model was built by standardizing categorical data and selecting the optimal K value (number of neighbors) for balanced accuracy. Each classification prediction depends on how close the test data is to its neighboring data points.
